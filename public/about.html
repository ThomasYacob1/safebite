About SafeBite

A food safety tool built to make FDA recall data accessible to everyone.

The Problem

Every year, the FDA issues hundreds of food recalls due to contamination, undeclared allergens, and safety breaches. While this data is publicly available, it is buried in government databases that are difficult to search, filter, or understand at a glance.

Most people purchase food without ever checking whether a product has been recalled. SafeBite closes that information gap by surfacing FDA recall data in a clean, searchable, and easy-to-understand format.

Who This Is For

  • Everyday grocery shoppers who want to stay informed
  • Families managing food allergies or sensitivities
  • Public health advisors tracking food safety trends
  • Small grocery stores monitoring their product inventory

How It Works

SafeBite connects directly to the openFDA Food Enforcement API, a government-verified data source updated with new recall reports on a rolling basis. Search results are pulled live so you are always seeing the most current data available.

Your recent searches are saved to our database so you can quickly revisit past lookups.

Understanding Recall Classes

Class I

The most serious type. There is a reasonable probability that consuming the product will cause serious adverse health consequences or death. Examples include E. coli, Listeria, or Salmonella contamination.

Class II

A situation where use of or exposure to a product may cause temporary, reversible adverse health consequences. Often includes undeclared allergens.

Class III

A situation where use of or exposure to a product is unlikely to cause any adverse health consequences. Examples include labeling errors or minor packaging issues.

Data Source

All recall data is sourced from the openFDA Food Enforcement API, a free public API maintained by the U.S. Food & Drug Administration.
